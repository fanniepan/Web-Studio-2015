INSTAGRAM

CASE STUDY

Instagram wants to diversify its revenue stream beyond just advertising by allowing users to customize and purchase physical versions of their photos. Identifying where in the app you will introduce this feature. Understanding how people edit and customize photos for printing. Design a flow for selecting a photo, and editing. Design the checkout and delivery flow. Design the key screens using the look and feel of Instagram.This was to be finished in one and a half weeks.

SOLUTION AND PROCESS

In order to introduce the new feature to the Instagram users, I had to come up with interview questions, and interview a group of Instagram users. By doing so, I was able to come up with different print options, and understand why they use Instagram to further strengthen my design. From my research, I concluded that Instagram users stick to Instagram due to the simplicity of the design of photo sharing. Eventually, I was able to come up with primary and secondary personas including their scenarios and stories. By making storyboards, I was able to sketch out designs and iterate on them until I came up with the final mock-up. Final prototype was made on Axure and all designs were made in Adobe Illustrator.

HIGHLIGHTS

SPOTLESS PURCHASE ICON INTEGRATION

Purchase prints icon is placed in the navigation bar of the Instagram user's page where users can purchase their own photos.

EASY PHOTO SELECTION

The "Print Photos" design was based off of the "Camera Roll" design on Version 6.14.0. This allows users to understand the functionality of the feature.

MULTIPLE PRINT OPTIONS

Options include different print sizes, borders, and quantities.

SIMPLE CHECKOUT PROCESS

The checkout is fast and easy to use with fast input options for billing and more.
